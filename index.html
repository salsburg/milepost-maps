Additional Datasets Used in Model

CONUS US Census City Populations

Interactive map of the 1,000 largest U.S. cities with 2020–2024 population and growth data. Compare shrinking metros like NYC, Chicago, and LA against high-growth Sunbelt cities. Population dynamics serve as a strong proxy for freight absorption and site selection when paired with highway networks and parking demand.

Open Map

Buried Storage Tanks (Fuel Sales Proxy)

Underground Storage Tank (UST) records provide a useful proxy for fuel sales activity. Mapping UST sites reveals fueling infrastructure beyond branded truck stops and helps cross-check supply density, identify underserved areas, and confirm local fueling patterns during site selection.

Open Map

Federal/State Public Parking (Alternatives)

Rest areas, welcome centers, weigh stations, and service plazas are essential relief points for drivers. Highlighted by Jason’s Law, these facilities underscore the nationwide shortage of safe truck parking and remain vital in corridors underserved by private stops.

Open Map

Dedicated Private Parking (Alternatives)

Privately operated yards and parking facilities provide secure, reservable capacity near freight hubs. With features like controlled access and flexible leasing, they reduce congestion at truck stops, support HOS compliance, and ensure smoother freight flow into urban markets.

Open Map
